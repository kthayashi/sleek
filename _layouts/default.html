Welcome to my website! I am a Ph.D. student in Dr. Nathan Kraft's Plant Community Ecology Lab in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at UCLA. I received my B.S. in Biology at Brown University, where I was a member of Dr. Dov Sax's Conservation Science Lab. My research interests are broadly in plant community ecology and biogeography. Some topics that I am interested in exploring include:

  1. The relative importance of different fundamental ecological processes (e.g. niche selection, ecological drift, dispersal) in determining community dynamics along ecological gradients and across spatial scales.
  2. How local coexistence mechanisms and regional community assembly processes can interact to jointly influence community structure and dynamics.
  3. Using functional traits and demographic processes to better understand species' climatic niches and geographic distributions.
I aim to explore these topics from the perspectives of functional traits, niche concepts, population dynamics, and metacommunity processes. Stay tuned as my research develops! I can also be found on Twitter, Google Scholar, ORCIDÂ®, and GitHub.
