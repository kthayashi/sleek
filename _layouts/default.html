I am a Ph.D. student in Dr. Nathan Kraft's Plant Community Ecology Lab in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at UCLA. My research interests are broadly in plant community ecology and biogeography. More specifically, my research currently focuses on:

  1. Disentangling the influences of niche selection (with a particular focus on competition) and ecological drift on species coexistence in California annual plant communities.
  2. Understanding how species coexistence and community assembly are mediated by species' functional traits and characteristics of the regional species pool.
  3. Assessing the effects of biotic and abiotic factors on the functional trait space and assembly of subalpine meadow communities along an elevational gradient in the Colorado Rocky Mountains.
Prior to starting at UCLA, I received my B.S. in Biology (Ecology and Evolutionary Biology Track) at Brown University, where I was a member of Dr. Dov Sax's Conservation Science Lab. I also worked with Elizabeth C. Miller and Dr. John J. Wiens (University of Arizona) on the global historical biogeography of percomorph fishes, and with Dr. Brian Enquist's research group (University of Arizona) at the Rocky Mountain Biological Laboratory on the functional structure of subalpine meadow communities along an elevational gradient.
