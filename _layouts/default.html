I am a Ph.D. student in Dr. Nathan Kraft's Plant Community Ecology Lab in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at UCLA. My research interests are broadly in plant community ecology and biogeography. More specifically, my current research focuses on characterizing how competitive interactions between annual plant species in a California grassland change along environmental gradients, with the aim of better understanding how spatial environmental heterogeneity maintains species diversity in natural communities. I am also hoping to extend this work to provide insights into how competition can influence species' geographic range dynamics under global environmental change.

Prior to starting at UCLA, I received my B.S. in Biology (Ecology and Evolutionary Biology Track) at Brown University, where I was a member of Dr. Dov Sax's Conservation Science Lab. I also worked with Elizabeth Miller and Dr. John Wiens (University of Arizona) on the global historical biogeography of percomorph fishes, and with Dr. Brian Enquist's research group (University of Arizona) at the Rocky Mountain Biological Laboratory on the functional structure of subalpine meadow communities along an elevational gradient.
